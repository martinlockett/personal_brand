# Martin Lockett — Personal Brand Guide

> [!quote] Motto
> *Vere Esse, Libere Agere, Alte Intellegere*
> **Be true · act freely · understand deeply**

This guide translates who you actually are — drawn from [[My values]] and [[Personality Summary]] — into a consistent visual and verbal identity. It is deliberately **quiet, deep, and considered**, not loud or performative. That is the whole point: the brand should reveal itself, not broadcast.

---

## 1. Brand essence

**The Observer who builds.**

A generative, people-aware thinker with a private interior. Ideas come prolifically; you read people well; you connect at depth with the right few. The brand should feel like someone who has *thought about it properly* — clear, grounded, unhurried, and warm to those who get close.

| Pillar | Rooted in | Shows up as |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Truth** | Authenticity, integrity | Plain language, no gloss, no hype |
| **Purpose** | Meaning over status | Everything has a reason; nothing decorative-only |
| **Freedom** | Creative autonomy | Original, unorthodox, made on your own terms |
| **Depth** | Understanding, close bonds | Layered, considered, rewards attention |

**Three words:** *Considered · Genuine · Generative.*

---

## 2. Logo & wordmark

Starting from scratch, the recommended system is restrained — a wordmark plus a monogram, no icon gimmickry (visibility-chasing is off-brand for you).

- **Primary wordmark:** `Martin Lockett` set in the display serif, letter-spaced lightly, lowercase or title case. Calm, classical, confident.
- **Monogram:** `ML` for compact use (avatar, favicon, stamp). Keep it a clean serif ligature — two letters that quietly interlock, not a logo trying too hard.
- **Signature mark (optional):** the motto in small caps beneath the wordmark for formal contexts.

> [!tip] Construction rules
> - Always give the mark generous clear space — whitespace *is* the brand.
> - Monochrome first: it must work in pure Ink or pure Parchment before any colour is added.
> - Never stretch, outline, add shadow, or place on a busy background.

## 3. Colour palette

A restrained five-colour system: deep and thoughtful, with a single warm spark for the generative, communicative side (the Gemini/Ideation energy).

| Swatch | Name | Hex | Role |
|--------|------|-----|------|
| <span style="display:inline-block;width:48px;height:18px;background:#1C2B40;border:1px solid #ccc"></span> | **Ink Navy** | `#1C2B40` | Primary. Depth, intellect, calm under pressure. Text & dark surfaces. |
| <span style="display:inline-block;width:48px;height:18px;background:#B8843E;border:1px solid #ccc"></span> | **Brass** | `#B8843E` | Accent. The spark — ideation, warmth, communication. Use sparingly. |
| <span style="display:inline-block;width:48px;height:18px;background:#4A6360;border:1px solid #ccc"></span> | **Slate Teal** | `#4A6360` | Secondary. Groundedness, balance. Supporting elements. |
| <span style="display:inline-block;width:48px;height:18px;background:#F3EEE4;border:1px solid #ccc"></span> | **Parchment** | `#F3EEE4` | Light background. Authentic, classical, paper-like. |
| <span style="display:inline-block;width:48px;height:18px;background:#23282E;border:1px solid #ccc"></span> | **Graphite** | `#23282E` | Body text on light. Softer than pure black — honest, not harsh. |

> [!info] Usage ratio
> Roughly **60% Parchment / 25% Ink Navy / 10% Slate Teal / 5% Brass.** Brass is a seasoning, never a base. If a layout feels loud, you've used too much Brass.

**Accessibility:** Ink Navy and Graphite both pass AA contrast on Parchment. Brass on Parchment is decorative only — never set body text in Brass.

## 4. Typography

A two-typeface system: a characterful serif for voice and authority, a humanist sans for clarity and warmth. Both are free (Google Fonts), so they work everywhere.

### Display & headings — *Fraunces* (serif)
Modern but classical, with personality in its detail — fits the Latin motto and the depth of thought. Use for the wordmark, headings, and pull-quotes.
*Alternative: Spectral or EB Garamond if you want something more reserved.*

### Body & UI — *Inter* (humanist sans)
Clean, neutral, highly legible. The humanist warmth keeps it from feeling corporate. Use for all running text, captions, and interface.
*Alternative: Source Sans 3.*

| Use | Typeface | Weight | Notes |
|-----|----------|--------|-------|
| Wordmark | Fraunces | Medium | Light letter-spacing |
| H1 / H2 | Fraunces | Semibold / Medium | Generous line height |
| Body | Inter | Regular | 16–18px, ~1.6 line height |
| Emphasis | Inter | Medium / Italic | Restraint over bold |
| Motto / captions | Fraunces Italic | Regular | The classical voice |

> [!tip] Type principle
> Plenty of air. Wide margins, generous leading, short measure. The layout should feel *unhurried* — a reader should sense there's room to think.

## 5. Tone of voice

How the brand sounds, drawn straight from your values.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Say it plainly and precisely | Hype, buzzwords, or salesy energy |
| Explain *why* — give the reasoning | Assert without substance |
| Warm, but not performative | Over-familiar or attention-seeking |
| Let depth show; reward attention | Dumb it down or pad it out |
| Comfortable with a considered pause | Rush to fill silence |

**Voice in one line:** *Thoughtful, honest, and quietly warm — the sound of someone who understood it before they said it.*

---

## 6. Imagery & texture

- **Mood:** natural light, paper and ink, muted tones, considered negative space. Quiet over busy.
- **Textures:** subtle paper grain, the Parchment tone, fine rules and lines rather than heavy blocks.
- **Photography:** real and unposed over polished stock. Depth of field, calm composition.
- **Avoid:** gloss, neon, stock-photo gestures, clutter, anything that performs.

---

## 7. Putting it together

> [!example] Quick application recipes
> **CV / portfolio header:** Parchment background · `Martin Lockett` wordmark in Ink Navy Fraunces · a single Brass hairline rule · body in Graphite Inter.
>
> **Email signature:** Name in Fraunces, role in Inter, one Brass character (·) as separator.
>
> **Avatar / favicon:** `ML` monogram, Parchment on Ink Navy.
>
> **Slide / document:** Ink Navy heading, Parchment field, Slate Teal for secondary callouts, Brass only to mark the one thing that matters on the page.

---

## 8. Golden rules

1. **Whitespace is the brand** — when in doubt, remove, don't add.
2. **Brass is a spark, not a fill** — 5% maximum.
3. **Plain truth over polish** — say the real thing, simply.
4. **Monochrome must work first** — colour is the last layer, never the crutch.
5. **Reward attention** — depth that reveals itself beats anything that shouts.
